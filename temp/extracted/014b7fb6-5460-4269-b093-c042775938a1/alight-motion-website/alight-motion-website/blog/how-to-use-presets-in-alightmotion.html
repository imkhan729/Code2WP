Make your videos look pro in minutes with presets, tips, and custom styles

Are you excited to make cool videos like the ones you see on YouTube or TikTok? Below, we’ll explain what presets are, how to use them, and how to create your own custom styles — with simple steps anyone can follow.

What Are AlightMotion Presets?

Imagine you’re coloring a picture — instead of picking each color one by one, you use a template with perfect shades. That’s a preset in AlightMotion: a saved combo of effects, colors, and styles you can apply with one tap. Presets can include:

  • Color correction and grading
  • Light effects like glow or lens flares
  • Film grain or texture for a vintage feel
  • Transitions and text animations

They save time and help you create professional‑looking videos — even if you’re just starting out.

Why Use Presets?

  • Save Time: Skip manual tweaks — get a polished look instantly.
  • Look Pro: Achieve studio‑style results, fast.
  • Be Creative: Try different styles and pick what fits your story.
  • Stay Consistent: Keep the same look across all your videos.

Step‑by‑Step: How to Use Presets

  1. Open AlightMotion → New Project. Choose a resolution (16:9 YouTube, 9:16 Reels/TikTok) and name your project.
  2. Import your video. Tap the + icon to add media and place it on the timeline.
  3. Open the Presets panel. Browse categories like Color, Effects, Transitions, and Text.
  4. Preview and apply. Tap a preset to preview; tap Apply if you like it.
  5. Adjust (optional). Tweak intensity, opacity, or duration. Use keyframes to animate over time.

New to animation? Learn keyframe basics in our 10 Pro Tips for AlightMotion and How to Add Text.

Pro Tips for Better Results

✓
Stack presets lightly: e.g., color grade + film grain + soft glow. Avoid overdoing it.
✓
Animate with keyframes: make glow pulse, ramp exposure, or time effects to music.
✓
Protect skin tones: reduce saturation slightly or isolate hues if faces look unnatural.
✓
Use preset transitions: Zoom Fade, Glitch Switch, or Blur Slide between clips.

How to Create Your Own Custom Presets

  1. Build your look: combine Curves, Levels, Vignette, Blur/Sharpen, light leaks, or grain.
  2. Tune the style: adjust contrast, hue, and warmth until it fits your brand.
  3. Save as Preset: tap “Save as Preset,” give it a clear name (e.g., Cinematic Warm).
  4. Test: apply to a new clip to verify it works broadly, not just one scene.

Organize and Save Your Presets

  • Create folders by theme: Cinematic, Vlog, Gaming, Music.
  • Backup custom presets (.alyp) to Google Drive for safe keeping.
  • Share with teammates by exporting and re‑importing your packs.
