What Is a QR Code?

Think of a QR code as a tiny square that holds a shortcut. When your phone scans it, it instantly opens a link to a website, video, file, or page — no typing long URLs. Perfect for sending people straight to your preset packs, tutorials, or a project import guide.

Why Use QR Codes with AlightMotion?

  • Skip non‑clickable captions: On platforms like Instagram, viewers can’t tap links — but they can scan.
  • Share instantly: Presets, tutorials, and full videos open right away.
  • Look professional: Add QR codes to thumbnails, Stories, or posters for a modern touch.

Step‑by‑Step: Make a QR Code for Your Project

  1. Export from AlightMotion: MP4 for video, GIF for loops, or copy a shareable link to your tutorial/preset page. Aim for high quality.
  2. Upload to the cloud: Use Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive. For Drive: upload → open file menu → Share → Copy link → set to “Anyone with the link can view”.
  3. Create a QR code: Use a reputable generator such as QRCode Monkey, QR Code Generator, or QR Stuff. Paste your link, customize, and download the PNG/SVG.
  4. Add to your design: In Canva, PicsArt, or AlightMotion, place the QR in a corner (bottom‑right works well) and add a CTA like “Scan to download”.
  5. Test it: Scan with 2–3 phones to ensure it’s readable.

Best Practices

  • Use strong contrast (black/white) and keep a clean border around the code.
  • Don’t stretch or blur the code; keep it at least 2 cm wide in the final image.
  • Make sure the destination page is fast and mobile‑friendly.
  • Use dynamic QR services if you need to change the destination later.

Creative Ways to Use QR Codes

  • Share preset packs: Zip 5 looks, upload, and link via QR — promote with "Free Neon Pack". See our Presets & QR Codes Guide.
  • Link to tutorials: Add a QR to your thumbnail that jumps to your full YouTube tutorial.
  • Portfolio hub: Point the QR to a simple Linktree or site with your best edits.
  • Client/school feedback: Share review links via QR for faster comments.
  • Cross‑promotion: “Scan to follow” CTAs for TikTok/Instagram.
